Types of Brain Strokes

Ischemic Stroke

A condition in which a blood vessel in the brain ruptures and causes bleeding inside the brain. This causes severe headache, tingling, or paralysis in face, arm, or leg, trouble swallowing, trouble with vision, loss of balance, loss of consciousness and confusion.

Hemmoragic Stroke

A type of stroke that occurs when a blood vessel supplying blood to the brain is obstructed, leading to a lack of blood flow and oxygen to part of the brain. This obstruction is typically caused by a blood clot or plaque buildup in the arteries.
